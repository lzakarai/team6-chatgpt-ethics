Real-World Applications

Education

One of the real-world examples of ChatGPT would be its usage in education. Students and teachers both use ChatGPT to help guide them with topics, concepts, and learning techniques. However, students utilize ChatGPT a lot because it helps them understand difficult subjects, explain confusing topics in simpler terms, get examples and learn through them. They also find assistance in brainstorming ideas, reviewing their writing, and studying for quizzes or exams. For example, if a student is struggling with a programming assignment, involving coding and complex structures, they would ask ChatGPT to either “explain the code”, “why am I getting this error?”, or “What steps should I redo?” This helps the students because ChatGPT is able to break the answers down step-by-step, serving as a tutor or a guide to the student. Additionally, research states that ChatGPT is commonly used in higher education and practical skill learning, where people often connect it with productivity, efficiency, and ethics (Mogavi et al., 2024). This creates an impact because students who do not have a tutor but need extra assistance can still get the support outside of class. On the other hand, teachers can also use ChatGPT as a learning tool to make lesson plans, create quiz questions, summarize readings, and explain harder topics in a simple way. However, the issue lies when students may start depending on ChatGPT too much, because of its easy accessibility, and instead of using it to learn, they can have an impact on their brain to not do the work, while they can finish the whole assignment using ChatGPT. This creates concern that ChatGPT may lead to superficial learning habits and reduce students’ sociability and critical thinking skills (Mogavi et al., 2024).

Customer Service and Business

The second example of ChatGPT can be found in the customer service and business areas. Companies can use ChatGPT-powered models on websites to answer customers’ questions quickly. For example, if a customer has an inquiry of “Where is my order?”, “How do I return this item?” or “What are the store hours?”, then instead of waiting for a human worker, a virtual chatbot assistant can respond right away. This is helpful, especially for commonly asked questions to reduce waiting time, and have automated responses through the chatbot, being available 24/7. ChatGPT can also help employees with writing their emails, summarizing customer complaints, and creating reports. Moreover, in healthcare and customer support settings, ChatGPT can help improve communication, provide personalized information, and support customers or patients more efficiently (Haleem et al., 2024). This is important because customer service is very important for trust, and if people can get their questions answered faster, they will feel more satisfied with the company. However, some challenges rely on misunderstanding the customer, giving outdated information, wrong answers, and even sounding confident while being incorrect. This can be risky because customers would trust the answer without knowing it could be incorrect.

Complexity and Social Challenges

The utilization of ChatGPT within education and healthcare depicts that while the technology can enhance productivity and easy information retrieval, it also produces essential social and real-world challenges. In universities, students commonly utilize ChatGPT for assignments, research support, and to study, while staff and faculty could utilize it for administrative or academic duties. However, one main problem is AI hallucinations, where the system produces inaccurate or false information. A study of a university community discovered that greater than half of participants had faced hallucinations, increasing worries about the trustworthiness of AI tools in educational environments (Kamel, 2025). This is crucial because incorrect information can hurt student learning, compromise critical thinking skills, and develop academic integrity if students depend on AI-generated responses without any validations. It also leads to challenges of using ChatGPT properly because users require understanding of AI, fact-checking practices, and a solid grasp of the tool’s constraints.

Similar obstacles show up in healthcare, where precision is even greater crucial. ChatGPT has the capability of supporting medical documentation, patient dialogue, and condensing intricate information. However, researchers confirmed that ChatGPT generated entirely made-up medical studies and incorrect references when asked questions related to clinical practices, despite the fact that the answers seemed credible (Siontis et al., 2023). These scenarios are important because inaccurate medical information may sway treatment decisions, lower assurance in healthcare systems, and possibly endanger patient safety. Utilizing ChatGPT successfully in healthcare is challenging because medical professionals need to cautiously evaluate outputs, implement professional judgment, and confirm that sensitive information stays protected. Overall, these examples depict that ChatGPT can be beneficial in practical environments, but accountable implementation demands human supervision, confirmation, and ethical understanding.
